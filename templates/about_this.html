What Is AQI ?
AQI (Air Quality Index) is a measure of the quality of air in a specific location. The AQI values ranges from 0-500, with higher values indicating poorer air quality. It is calculated based on the levels of airborne pollutants present in the atmosphere such as SO2, CO2, CO, NO2, ozone.

Pollution, whether from vehicle emissions, industrial activities, or natural sources like wildfires, significantly impacts air quality and human health. AQI provides a standardized way to assess pollution levels, helping authorities and individuals make informed decisions to mitigate exposure. Regular monitoring of AQI allows for timely interventions such as issuing air quality alerts, implementing emission controls, and promoting public awareness about reducing activities that contribute to pollution. Ultimately, efforts to reduce pollution not only improve air quality but also safeguard ecosystems and promote sustainable living for future generations.
Some Of The Assumptions We Have Made Are:
  • We will be ignoring the effects of aerosols as the available data is very little.
  • We assume the data acquired during the period of the COVID pandemic to be an outlier, as it was an unanticipated global event and caused unpredictable patterns of pollution and energy consumption.
  • We assume that the model is capable of accommodating increasing data volumes and user interactions.
  • Access to real-time air quality data is essential for accurate predictions.
  • We assume the solution will be cost effective to ensure sustainability.
